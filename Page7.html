Appreciate Heritage at Camina Balay nga Bato

Camina Balay nga Bato, Osmena Street, Villa Arevalo District, Iloilo City

Camina Balay Nga Bato, a charming ancestral home in Iloilo City, stands as one of the city's premier tourist attractions. Built between 1860 and 1865, its name translates to "home beside the river," reflecting its riverside location. Originally the modest dwelling of Femundo Avancena and Eulalia Abaja, the house draws inspiration from the traditional bahay kubo design, showcasing enduring architectural beauty despite upgrades and alterations over the years.

For enthusiasts of vintage charm, Camina Balay Nga Bato offers a captivating journey into the past. Adorned with altars, pottery, relics, antique furniture, and vintage photographs, the house serves as a visual delight. On the ground floor, weavers operate looms, crafting fabrics that are sold onsite. Visitors can also purchase handicrafts such as shawls, patadyongs (traditional skirts), and slippers, adding to the allure of this historic gem.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Camina Balay Nga Bato is during its opening hours from 8 AM to 5 PM. Being an indoor site, you can explore it comfortably throughout the year. However, it's advisable to avoid the typhoon season, which usually occurs from June to August, to ensure a pleasant experience. For a truly unforgettable visit, consider traveling to Iloilo City and Camina Balay Nga Bato in early January to coincide with the city's celebrated Dinagyang Festival.
