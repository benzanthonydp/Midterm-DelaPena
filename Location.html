Location

Soil Type

Iloilo City occupies an alluvial plain primarily composed of river and coastal deposits from the Holocene period. The soil profile and sedimentary environment confirm multiple layers, leading to the classification of the area into two sedimentary basins: the Jaro sedimentary basin and the Iloilo sedimentary basin.

Slope

Iloilo City exhibits the typical characteristics of an alluvial plain, with a relatively flat terrain similar to many other coastal cities in the Philippines. Covering over 83% of its total land area, the city's landscape features slopes of less than 3 degrees. Only a small portion, approximately 2.26% of the land area, has elevations exceeding 8%. This flat topography makes the entire city suitable for urban development, with residential, commercial, institutional, and industrial buildings widely distributed across the area.

.

Specifically, 83.87% of Iloilo City's land falls within the slope category of less than 3%, while 5.23% is categorized within the 3-8% slope range. The remaining 2.26% consists of areas with slopes greater than 8%. Overall, the city is deemed suitable for urban use throughout its entirety.
