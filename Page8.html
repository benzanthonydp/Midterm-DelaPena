Parish of Saint Anne in Molo

Location: San Pedro Street, Molo, Iloilo City

Saint Anne Parish Church stands as one of the most iconic landmarks in Iloilo City, distinguished by its striking neo-Gothic architecture. Notably, the church is recognized for its distinctive pyramidal red spires crowning the summits of its bell towers, adding to its allure and making it a prominent feature of the city's skyline.

Due to the unavailability of cement during its construction, masons utilized a mixture of sand and egg whites to bind the white coral pebbles and limestones composing the chapel. Interestingly, the tradition of Ilonggo pastry treats is said to have originated with the Molo women, who crafted baked pastries using egg yolks. Among these treats are the renowned locally made Spanish-inspired delicacies like Galletas (thin biscuits), Baadas (sugar-glazed cookies), and Barquillos (rolled wafer biscuits), which continue to be preserved and enjoyed today at Panaderia de Molo.
