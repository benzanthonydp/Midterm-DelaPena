National Museum - Western Visayas

Bonifacio Drive. City Proper 5000 Iloilo City, Philippines

The National Museum Western Visayas Iloilo Provincial Jail, originally constructed in 1911, stands as one of the earliest American-built structures in the Philippines. Also known as the Iloilo Rehabilitation Center (IRC) or Prison of Iloilo, it served as a jail until 2006. Situated within the Iloilo Provincial Capitol Complex on Bonifacio Drive in City Proper, Iloilo City, the building spans approximately 860 square meters.

Recognizing its exceptional architectural, cultural, and historical significance, the National Museum of the Philippines (NMP) declared the Iloilo Provincial Jail an Important Cultural Property (ICP) on December 16, 2016. The design of the jail is credited to Architect William Parsons, who served as the Consulting Architect of the Philippine Government and was responsible for designing various public buildings and parks across the Philippine Islands. Parsons adhered to the principle of "form follows function" in his original design, reflecting the ethos of Modern architecture.
